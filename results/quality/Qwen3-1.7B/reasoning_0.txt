We are told that a snail climbs **3 feet during the day** and **slides back 2 feet at night**, and the wall is **30 feet high**.

---

### Step-by-step:

1. **Net gain per day**:
   - The snail climbs 3 feet during the day.
   - Then it slides back 2 feet at night.
   - So, the net gain per day is:
     $$
     3 - 2 = 1 \text{ foot per day}
     $$

2. **But on the final day**, the snail doesn't slide back. That is, if the snail reaches or exceeds the top during the day, it doesn't slide back.

3. **So, in the last day**, the snail only needs to climb 3 feet to reach the top.

---

### Let's calculate how many days it takes:

- Each day, the snail makes a net gain of 1 foot.
- After 27 days, the snail has climbed 27 feet.
- On the 28th day, the snail climbs 3 feet, which brings it to:
  $$
  27 + 3 = 30 \text{ feet}
  $$

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

**28 days**.
