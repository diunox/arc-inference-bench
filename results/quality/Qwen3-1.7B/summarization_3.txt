HTTPS is crucial for secure communication over the internet because it encrypts data transmitted between a user's browser and a server, preventing eavesdropping, man-in-the-middle attacks, and data tampering. It uses the Transport Layer Security (TLS) protocol to establish a secure, encrypted connection. During the TLS handshake, both the client and server agree on a secure encryption algorithm, exchange cryptographic keys, and establish a shared secret for encrypting data. This process involves several steps, including the exchange of digital certificates, key exchange algorithms, and the establishment of a symmetric session key. HTTPS ensures that data remains confidential and intact, making it essential for protecting sensitive information such as passwords, credit card details, and personal data.
