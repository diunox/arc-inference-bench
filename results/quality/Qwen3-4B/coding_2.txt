Here's a bash one-liner that does exactly what you asked:

```bash
du -hSk /var/log/* --max-depth=1 | sort -hrk 1 | head -n 10
```

### Explanation:

- `du -hSk /var/log/* --max-depth=1`:  
  - `du` (disk usage) lists file sizes.
  - `-h` for human-readable sizes.
  - `-S` shows only sizes (not total sizes of directories).
  - `-k` sorts by size (this is the default, but specified for clarity).
  - `--max-depth=1` limits the search to files directly in `/var/log`, not subdirectories.
  - `/var/log/*` lists all files in `/var/log`.

- `sort -hrk 1`:  
  - `-h` for human-readable sorting (interprets sizes like 1K, 200M, etc.).
  - `-r` reverses the sort (descending).
  - `-k 1` sorts by the first field (file size).
  
- `head -n 10`: shows only the top 10 largest files.

This will output the 10 largest files in `/var/log` by size, in human-readable format, sorted from largest to smallest.
