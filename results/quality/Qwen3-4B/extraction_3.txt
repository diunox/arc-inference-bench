Sure! Here's a Docker Compose YAML snippet that matches your requirements:

```yaml
version: '3.8'

services:
  db:
    image: postgres:16
    container_name: mydb
    environment:
      POSTGRES_USER: app
      POSTGRES_PASSWORD: secret123
    ports:
      - "5433:5432"
    volumes:
      - mydb_data:/var/lib/postgresql/data
    deploy:
      resources:
        memory: 512M

volumes:
  mydb_data:
```

### Explanation:
- **`image: postgres:16`**: Uses PostgreSQL 16 image.
- **`environment`**: Sets the PostgreSQL user and password.
- **`ports`**: Maps the container's PostgreSQL port (5432) to port 5433 on the host.
- **`volumes`**: Uses a volume for persistent storage.
- **`deploy.resources.memory`**: Limits the container to 512MB of memory.
- **`container_name`**: Names the container `mydb`.
