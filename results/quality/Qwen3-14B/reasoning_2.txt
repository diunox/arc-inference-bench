This is a classic logic puzzle. Let's break it down step by step.

### Given:
- There are **three boxes**.
- Each box is **labeled**: **Apples**, **Oranges**, and **Mixed**.
- **All labels are wrong** (i.e., **no box has the correct label**).
- You can **pick one fruit from one box**.
- Based on that, you must **determine which box contains what**.

---

### Goal:
Determine **which box to pick from** to correctly identify the contents of all boxes.

---

### Key Insight:
Since **all labels are wrong**, we can deduce the following:

- The **Mixed** box **cannot** be mixed — it must be **all apples** or **all oranges**.
- The **Apples** box **cannot** be all apples — it must be **oranges** or **mixed**.
- The **Oranges** box **cannot** be all oranges — it must be **apples** or **mixed**.

---

### Strategy:
The trick is to **pick from the box labeled "Mixed"**.

### Why?

Because:
- Since **all labels are wrong**, the **"Mixed"** box **cannot** be mixed — it must be **all apples or all oranges**.
- Let’s say you **pick one fruit** from the **Mixed** box.

#### Case 1: You pick an **apple** from the Mixed box.
- That means the **Mixed** box is **actually all apples**.
- So the label "Mixed" is wrong → it’s really **Apples**.
- The label "Apples" is also wrong, so it cannot be apples → it must be **Oranges** or **Mixed**.
- The label "Oranges" is also wrong → it can’t be oranges → it must be **Apples** or **Mixed**.

Now we know:

- **Mixed** box is actually **Apples** (since we picked an apple from it).
- That means the **Apples** box cannot be apples → it must be **Oranges** or **Mixed**.
- But **Mixed** is already apples, so the **Apples** box must be **Oranges**.
- Therefore, the **Oranges** box must be **Mixed**.

#### Case 2: You pick an **orange** from the Mixed box.
- Then the **Mixed** box is actually **Oranges**.
- The **Oranges** label is wrong → it can’t be oranges → it must be **Apples** or **Mixed**.
- The **Apples** label is wrong → it can’t be apples → it must be **Oranges** or **Mixed**.
- Then you can deduce the rest accordingly.

---

### ✅ Conclusion:
**Pick a fruit from the box labeled "Mixed"**.

This is the **correct choice** because:
- The **Mixed** box is guaranteed to be **all apples or all oranges**, so picking from it gives you definite information.
- Once you know what's in that box, you can deduce the rest based on the incorrect labels.

---

### ✅ Final Answer:
**Pick a fruit from the box labeled "Mixed"**, because it is guaranteed to be either all apples or all oranges (not mixed), and that gives you the key to identifying all boxes.
