We are told:

- The snail climbs **3 feet during the day**.
- It **slides back 2 feet at night**.
- The wall is **30 feet high**.
- We need to find how many **days** it takes for the snail to reach the top.

---

### Step-by-step analysis:

Let’s think about the snail’s net progress each full **day-night cycle**:

- **Day**: Climbs 3 feet.
- **Night**: Slides back 2 feet.
- **Net gain per day**: $ 3 - 2 = 1 $ foot per day.

However, this is only true **until the snail reaches or exceeds the top**.

---

### Important observation:

Once the snail reaches or surpasses the **top of the wall (30 feet)** during the **day**, it **doesn't slide back** that night. So the final climb doesn't include the slide.

---

### Let's calculate:

Let’s assume the snail reaches the top on the **n-th day**.

Before the last day, the snail must be at a height of:

$$
\text{Height before last day} = 30 - 3 = 27 \text{ feet}
$$

Because on the last day, it climbs the final 3 feet to reach the top.

Now, how many days does it take to reach 27 feet, given that it gains 1 foot per day (net)?

$$
\text{Days to reach 27 feet} = 27 \text{ feet} ÷ 1 \text{ foot/day} = 27 \text{ days}
$$

On the **28th day**, the snail climbs the final 3 feet to reach the top.

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

$$
\boxed{28 \text{ days}}
$$

---

### Summary:

- For the first 27 days, the snail gains 1 foot per day (net).
- On the **28th day**, it climbs 3 feet and reaches the top without sliding back.

**Answer: 28 days**.
