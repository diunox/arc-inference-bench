HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) is crucial for ensuring secure communication over the internet, particularly when sensitive data like passwords, credit card numbers, and personal information are transmitted. HTTPS employs Transport Layer Security (TLS) or its predecessor, Secure Sockets Layer (SSL), to establish a secure connection between the client (such as a web browser) and the server. During the TLS handshake, the client and server engage in a process of mutual authentication. The server verifies its identity to the client, and the client checks the server's identity to ensure it is who it claims to be. The handshake also includes the negotiation of encryption keys for encrypting and decrypting data exchanged during the session. This process helps prevent eavesdropping, tampering, and impersonation attacks, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of data transmitted over the internet.
